Opportunities

We know college can be expensive, which is why we have various on-campus job opportunities for our students. Along with that career opportunities in the form of jobs, internships,study abroad etc. are an excellent way to display oneâ€™s talent and interest to potential employers- which is what the Center for Career Discovery and Development works on, bridging the gap between employers and Georgia Tech students.

Internships
  • Students can gain job experience for one semester, anywhere in the world.
  • Internships are single-semester, major-related work experiences that help students better understand the work environment and the applications of their studies.
  • These internships can be full-time or part-time and can be paid or unpaid.
    • Full-time: 40 hours/week
    • Part-time: 20 hours/week: Keep in mind that international students interning in the US have to register their internship with the school (obtain CPT/OPT). See (g) for more information regarding international student requirements.
    • OIE also supports a number of institutions around the world in promoting global internship programs. For these programs, students will not be required to apply for CPT/OPT.
Co-op program
  • Students work at least three alternating semesters in a paid position with the same company.
  • Students who successfully complete the program receive a co-op designation on their degree.

Career Advising
  • Center for Career Discovery and Development (C2D2) (C2D2)

    • C2D2 hold the annual career fair as well as workshops on resume building, interviewing, etc
    • Major-specific academic advisors
    • Upper-classmen with experience in desired field
Career Fair
  • Held in Fall and Spring
  • For internships, co-ops, and full-time positions
  • Some schools also host major-specific career fairs
On-Campus Jobs
  • TAs (Teaching Assistant) help professors with grading and running the class
  • RAs (Resident Advisor) are upperclassmen that live in dorms to build community and provide support for residents. They receive a rent credit and stipend every semester.
  • Student Assistants are always being hired by various departments on campus for office assistance and more.
  • Research Assistants are hired by labs to contribute to the research being conducted by faculty and graduate students.
  • Student Tutors help their peers on course material they may be struggling with.
  • Student Center - various jobs
  • Campus Recreation Center
Things to Consider as an International Student
  • Not all companies accept international interns so it is important to do your research before applying.
  • Again, any internship you take will need to be registered to maintain your visa status.
  • To intern legally, the work you do must be in line with your area of study
  • Registering an internship also requires that you apply for CPT/OPT. More information on this can be found on the OIE Website.
