Housing

First-Year Experience
When you register for on-campus housing, you will be asked if you want to opt-into this program. If you do, you will be required to live in first-year residence halls and purchase a meal plan. You will live on a floor with other first-years and your RAs (Resident Assistants) will host hall meetings and help you get accustomed to your new home at Tech. They will also be there to answer questions and direct you to any resources you might need.

East vs. West Campus
East Campus Housing West Campus Housing
East Campus is closer to first-year classes, the stadium, MARTA, fraternities and sororities, and Tech Square. West Campus is closer to the Campus Recreation Centre, athletic fields, and certain departmental buildings (Howey, Bioquad, MRDC).
Brittain and North Ave Dining Halls (buffet style) West Village Dining Hall, Wingzone
The first-year residence halls on East are: Howell, Harrison, Cloudman, Smith, Brown, Towers, Glenn, Field, Hanson, Hopkins, Matheson, Perry (dorm style) The other first-year residence halls on West campus are Freeman, Montag, Fitten, Folk, Caldwell, Hefner, Fulmer, and Woodruff.

East campus is livelier due to Greek life and Midtown, and the population is mostly first-years

Living Off-Campus

Some students move off-campus after their first year. Below are some of the most popular options near campus:

  • M Street
  • University House
  • Signature West Midtown
  • SQ5
  • Westmar
  • Arium Westside
  • The Standard
Living and Learning Communities
Living Learning Communities (LLC’s) are another type of special housing program first-years can apply to. There are many different types of LLC’s you can join, each with an academic and/or social focus.
