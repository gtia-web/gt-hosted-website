Extracurricular Life

Contrary to popular belief, Tech student life expands beyond just the rigorous study and hours of research. Here’s what Tech has to offer to the non-academic aspect of student life.

Engage
Georgia Tech has over 400 student organizations that cater to the interests of its diverse student body. Engage is a website where you can find a description of each organization and the contact details for its Executive Board.

Greek Life
Approximately 37% of our student population is Greek, which means they are affiliated with a sorority or fraternity. Greek organizations serve a social purpose and act as a strong community for those involved.

Sports and Recreation
There are a wide variety of organizations centered around sports. Teams play at different levels (intramurals, varsity etc.) and many do not require any prior experience to join! You can learn more here about Georgia Tech’s formal Athletics Program here!

First-Year Leadership Organizations (FLO’s)
A FLO is a freshmen-led organization with strong GT departmental connections that seeks to better the Georgia Tech community through a unique program that enriches the first-year experience for its members and the entire freshman class. Joining a FLO is an opportunity to get involved in leadership positions early on in your Georgia Tech career. Here are the current FLO programs available:
  • Emerging Leaders (EL)
  • FreShGA
  • First-Year Activities Board (FAB)
  • First-Year Leadership Initiative (FLI)
  • Suit Up Professional Preparation (SUPP)
Cultural Organizations
Georgia Tech takes pride in its diverse student body. Joining a cultural organization can also help students find community and feel a sense of belonging in our large university. The Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers and India Club are currently two of the biggest cultural organizations at Tech that celebrate their respective regional cultures. Interested in joining GTIA? We recruit new ambassadors every Fall and Spring, so look out for our application during the beginning of each semester!

Major-Related and Professional Organizations
Many organizations also have an academic and/or professional focus. These organizations allow you to obtain valuable experience in an area you might want to work in later on. Some notable ones include Engineers Without Borders (EWB), Bits of Good, Undergraduate Research Ambassadors, Consult Your Community, and Student Competition Center Teams. However there are many more that cater to a very wide variety of interests.
