Transitioning To Tech

Let’s delve into what’s it like to be a Georgia Tech student.

New Student Transition Programs

  • FASET is the Freshman Acculturation to the Surroundings and Environs of Tech. It is an orientation to Georgia Tech
  • The Hive Electronics makerspace with electronics benchtops and equipment, 3D printers, laser cutters, and a machine shop.
  • Multimedia Studio Facility in the library that provides Adobe Creative Suite Master Collection, Final Cut Pro, and poster printing resources.
  • Paper & Clay Hosts hands-on activities including poster and banner printing, pottery, ceramics, sewing, and stained glass.
  • DramaTech DramaTech is Georgia Tech’s student-run theatre. Each season, DramaTech offers a full season of plays and musicals as well as a variety show.
  • Under the CouchUnder The Couch is a student-run music venue on the Georgia Tech Campus.

Safety

The Georgia Tech Police Department (GTPD) is a State agency that ensures the protection of all individuals on campus. They perform continuous patrolling round the clock as well as during early hours in certain areas of campus. Students are also encouraged to download the LiveSafe app which provides quick access to emergency features, location sharing with the GTPD and safety alerts and notifications.

Creativity on Campus

  • Invention Studio - Maker space for all Georgia Tech students. Has a machine shop (metal, wood, plastic), 3D printers, and waterjets. The use of the Invention Studio is free to all students and it is located in the MRDC.
  • Multimedia Studio - Facility in the library where computers have The Adobe Creative Suite Master Collection, Final Cut Pro. In addition there are plotters to print posters.
  • Paper & Clay - Hands on activities location on campus! Paper & Clay features poster & banner printing, pottery, ceramics, sewing and stained glass.
  • DramaTech - DramaTech is Georgia Tech’s student run theatre. It offers a theatrical laboratory environment at Georgia Tech that enriches the greater Atlanta community through innovative and diverse live performances. Each season, DramaTech offers a full season of plays, musicals as well as a variety show.
  • Under the Couch - Under The Couch is a student-run music venue on the Georgia Tech Campus.

Banks

There are many banks in the local area that offer checking and/or savings accounts designed for college students. These accounts often feature lower or no monthly fees, lower minimum balance requirements, etc. Make sure to consider the following before choosing a bank:

  • What are the fees for wiring money from overseas?
  • What is the minimum balance required? Is there a penalty if my balance goes below the minimum?
  • Will my checking account earn any interest?
  • What will I pay in monthly service charges?
  • Are there branches or ATM machines near Georgia Tech and where I live?

ATMs for Bank of America, SunTrust, and Wells Fargo are currently in the Student Center. These banks also have physical locations on or near campus.

Social Security Numbers (SSNs)

A Social Security Number (SSN) is a permanent nine-digit identification number administered by an agency of the U.S. federal government. SSNs are required for employment in the U.S. Eligibility:

  • F-1 students who are employed are eligible for a SSN.
  • J visa holders are eligible for a Social Security Number whether employed or not.

Obtaining an SSN:

  • OIE is well equipped to help you with this process. You can visit their office to talk to an advisor that can explain the relevant requirements and regulations.

Phones

There are several phone service providers near campus that can help you set up a phone plan. Ensure you carry valid forms of ID when visiting the store. The most popular providers are AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon.

Immigration Status

OIE provides several resources to ensure that students are aware of the requirements to maintain immigration status. This is a delicate matter that you should be aware of - attend any mandatory informational sessions, read emails, and schedule appointments with an OIE advisor if you have any questions. Detailed information can be found on their website.
