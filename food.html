Food

On Campus Dining

Our community restaurants are:
  • North Avenue (near North Avenue Apartments)
  • Brittain (near Brown and Smith residence halls)
  • West Village (near Freeman, Fulmer, and Fitten residence halls)
North Avenue and Brittain are traditional dining halls with an “all you can eat” option, while West Village consists of several mini-restaurants that serve different cuisines.

The Student Center houses a food court and a couple of retail dining stores including Chick-fil-A, Subway, and Panda Express.

Pro Tip: Use the Grubhub app to order food from these restaurants in advance so you can avoid waiting in long lines!

You can purchase food at these restaurants using cash or dining dollars (see below). In addition, there are a few restaurants (ex. Auditions, Southern Kitchen) here where you can use meal swipes to get a combo.

Off-Campus Dining

Due to Georgia Tech’s optimal location in downtown Atlanta, there are numerous dining options available within walking distance from campus. You can find some of GTIA’s favorite restaurants here!

Delivery Options

Services such as Uber Eats, Grubhub, and Doordash are used frequently on campus. There are also services that deliver home-cooked Indian and Chinese food to campus for pickup. Each of these services have their own group chats, in which you can join to place orders and then pick up on campus.

Meal Plans

All incoming first-year students that decide to live in on-campus housing are required to purchase a meal plan. If you join Greek Life, you must purchase the Greek meal plan (Greek 100). If you are second-year or above, there is a wider variety of meal plans that you can purchase to suit your needs. More details about the different kinds of plans can be found here<.

Meal Swipes

Meal swipes can be used at any of the community restaurants (Brittain, North Avenue, West Village). You can also use these at certain restaurants in the Student Center to purchase a combo meal. Meal swipes expire at the end of each semester.

Dining Dollars

Dining dollars are loaded onto your Buzzcard, and can be used at any on-campus dining option. Dining dollars are tax-exempt. At the end of the Spring semester, any unused dining dollars expire.

Grocery Options

If you prefer to cook your own meals at home, there are a number of grocery stores near campus where you can purchase everything you need:
  • Publix/Walmart/Kroger/Whole Foods/Target: These are some of the most popular grocery chains in the US. Publix, Whole Foods, and Target are all within walking distance from campus.
  • Patel Brothers (Indian Groceries): Patel Brothers is a prominent Indian grocery chain in the US. The closest one is 20 minutes away from campus.
  • H-Mart (East Asian Groceries): H-Mart is a 15-minute drive away. They offer a wide variety of food items from different East Asian countries.
