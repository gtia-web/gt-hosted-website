Academic Life

We know college is hard, especially a technical institute like Georgia Tech, but we which is why we have academic resources on campus to help with the transition.

Research And VIP Programs

  • Research: Georgia Tech offers a variety of research opportunities to undergraduate students. The most popular way to get involved in research is to work as an Undergraduate Research Assistant at a lab, either for pay or for academic credit. Students can also apply for the President’s Undergraduate Research Award. See more information here. Students interested in learning how to write a thesis often take up the Research Option. See more information here.
  • VIP Programs: The Vertically Integrated Projects (VIP) Program allows undergraduate students to contribute to a faculty-led research project in a team-based context. Undergraduate VIP students earn academic credits, while faculty and graduate students benefit from the design/discovery efforts of their teams. See more information here.
  • International students may not be eligible to join some research labs, for example, those working on government projects.

Study Abroad

  • Study Abroad Program Finder.
  • Georgia Tech offers two kinds of Study Abroad programs: Faculty Led and Exchange.
  • Popular programs include Georgia Tech Lorraine, Oxford Summer Program, EU Brussels Summer Program, Pacific Program. There are general study abroad programs and ones that cater to specific majors as well.
  • While doing a Georgia Tech study abroad program, students pay in-state tuition plus small fees.

General Academics

  • Courseoff is an online website that enables students to plan their schedules ahead of registration and simplifies the registration process. Besides offering a color coded scheme for various classes, it enables the students to select any particular class offered in the current/future semester, view the timings and class capacity, professors teaching those classes, and the course CRNs. CRN numbers are uniquely assigned to each class which can later be inputted during registration to quickly get the desired classes.
  • Students can also view the reviews and ratings of different professors for multiple subjects based on student feedback. The two commonly used sites are RateMyProfessor and Course Critique. Course Critique also has information regarding class sizes and grade distribution (GPA) for different courses over a span of 5-10 years. Georgia Tech students reference these tools to gauge a preliminary understanding of the course and how it might go, but it isn’t representative of everything the classes and professors entail.
  • The course scheduling at Georgia Tech is a process that spans 1-2 months. Any student may plan a four year schedule by looking at the Course Catalog. This lists all the requirements that a student must fulfill in addition to his/her major requirements to complete the degree. Students can reach out to their Academic Advisors for assistance in planning their course load for the following semester.

Tutoring/Advising

There are numerous tutoring resources available on campus which aid a student to succeed in his/her coursework. The Center for Academic Success located in the Clough Building offers many such opportunities.

  • One to One Tutoring is a completely free appointment based program offered to all students mostly at the 1000 and 2000 level.
  • OMED The Office of Minority Educational Development (OMED) offers study sessions for classes that are smaller and topic-specific.
  • PLUS Peer-Led Undergraduate Study (PLUS) sessions are held for 1000 and 2000 level math and science courses. They are taught by students that have previously taken the class and received an A.
  • Help DeskTutoring is available usually on all weekdays in introductory Calculus, Physics, Computer Science, and Chemistry courses. The Help Desk is run by the Teaching Assistants (TA) of that particular course.
  • Office Hours: All professors hold office hours, which are specific timings set by the professor outside of class time that students can use to clarify doubts, ask questions, etc. For some subjects, the TAs also hold their own office hours. The schedule of Office Hours is given by the professor or the TA on the first/second day of class and can usually be found on the syllabus.
  • LAP The Learning Assistance Program (LAP) is offered by Georgia Tech Housing and is a free, drop-in based tutoring available on most weekday evenings in several first year dorms.
  • Communication Center The Communication Lab provides assistance to students on assignments or projects relating to communication, as well as on cover letters, resumes, interview preparation etc.
  • Advising: Every student is assigned a major advisor who offers advising related to class class registration, internships/co-ops, and undergraduate research. In addition , students are also assigned individual academic advisors whom they can consult on a regular basis. Students are encouraged to meet with their advisor at least twice per semester.

Credits And Placement

Georgia Tech accepts transfer credit for Advanced Placement (AP) and International Baccalaureate (IB), Online courses, and courses that are taken at other accredited colleges and universities in the United States and Canada based on the transfer credit policy.
