Maintaining F1/J1 Student Status

Phones

Bank Account

There are many banks in the local area that offer several types of savings and checking accounts. Many banks have checking and/or savings accounts designed for college students, which often feature lower or no monthly fees, lower minimum balance requirements, etc.

Be sure to research different options to find the choice that best fits your needs. Make sure to consider the following before choosing a bank:
  1. What are the fees for wiring money from overseas?
  2. What is the minimum balance required? Is there a penalty if my balance goes below the minimum?
  3. Will my checking account earn any interest?
  4. What will I pay in monthly service charges?
  5. Are there branches or ATM machines near Georgia Tech and where I live?
Banks Around Campus
Wells Fargo: ATM in Student Center and Branch beside Student Center. Bank Of America: ATM in Student Center. SunTrust: ATM in Student Center

Open a Bank Account
In order to open a bank account, you should bring the following documents:

  • Georgia Tech ID (Buzzcard)
  • I-20 or DS-2019
  • Passport and I-94
You may be asked for a Social Security Number, but it is not required to open an account.

Social Security Number
International students do not require Social Security Number if they are not employed.

  • F-1 students who are employed are eligible for a Social Security Number.
  • F-2s are not eligible for a Social Security Number.
  • J visa holders are eligible for a Social Security Number whether employed or not. If a J visa holder is not employed, OIE would encourage you to think carefully about whether you would like to apply for an SSN because of some dangers of having one.
